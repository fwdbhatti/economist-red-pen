# Sybill — Sales Commission Percentages: Your Comp Plan Shouldn't Require a PhD to Understand

## Industry commission benchmarks (verbatim)

- Technology and SaaS: 10% commission on new ARR, range 5-20%
- Financial Services: 7-15% commission. 20-40% first year for insurance/wealth management, 5-10% trail on renewals
- **Manufacturing and Industrial Sales: 5-12% commission, typically with longer sales cycles**
- Real Estate: 5-6% residential, 4-8% commercial
- Pharmaceutical and Medical Device: 5-10%; medical devices 8-15% on capital equipment, 5-10% on consumables
- Retail and Consumer Goods: 3-8%

## OTE and base/variable splits (verbatim)

- Entry-Level AE (0-2 years): $80,000-$120,000 OTE, typically **60/40 or 50/50** base-to-variable split
- Mid-Level AE (2-5 years): $120,000-$180,000 OTE, often **50/50** split, sometimes **40/60** for new business hunters
- Senior AE/Enterprise AE (5+ years): $180,000-$300,000+ OTE, ranging from **40/60 to 30/70** base-to-commission splits
- Quota-to-OTE ratio: typically 4-6x; early-stage 3-4x; mature 5-7x

## Commission structure types covered

Base + Commission, Tiered, Revenue + Accelerators, Draw Against Commission, Straight Commission. The article gives worked examples in USD throughout.

## Role-specific commission rates

- New Business AEs (Hunters): 10-15%
- Account Executives with Account Management: 8-12% on new business, 5-8% on expansion
- Territory AEs: 7-10%
- Channel AEs: 5-8%

## Key performance datapoint

"Only 33% of sales professionals expect to hit quota."
Standard quota attainment expectation: 60-70% of team hitting target regularly.

## Notes on this source vs. the draft article

- The draft article cites Sybill (with "Iris AI") as the source for the claim that "70/30 base-to-OTE split is the most common structure in manufacturing." **The Sybill article does not say this.** Sybill's published splits are 60/40 or 50/50 for entry-level, 50/50 (or 40/60) for mid-level, and 40/60 to 30/70 for senior — none of which match a 70/30 manufacturing benchmark. The article does say industrial commission is 5-12%.
- The draft article cites Sybill for "30% of OTE is commission" — Sybill's mid-level standard is 50/50 (i.e. 50% commission), and senior is 60-70% commission. So the draft's 30% commission share runs *lower* than Sybill's published figures, not in line with them.
- All figures in Sybill are USD; no UK-specific or GBP figures appear.
- Sybill mentions only "33% expect to hit quota" — not a year-1 ramp benchmark. The draft's "60% Year 1 attainment" is sourced to "Bridge Group / Alba Talent", not Sybill.
