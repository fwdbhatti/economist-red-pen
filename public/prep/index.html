Liz Goulding CPO

Tests: vision · editorial sensitivity · cultural fit · commercial instinct.
How does this connect to subscriber experience?
The Red Pen protects the trust layer between your AI and 1.5M subscribers. Every AI feature — summaries, recommendations, copilot answers — needs this guardrail before it reaches a reader.
How would you prioritise this against everything else?
I wouldn't ship this first. Start by understanding what product teams are blocked on today. But if editorial trust is as central as your ProductCon talk suggests, a shared eval layer is foundational — everything else is safer once it exists.
What did you learn about our editors?
I haven't spoken to your editors. The prototype is based on publicly available writing and editorial principles. Phase 1 is specifically to fix that — shadow editors, understand where this fits or doesn't.
Why build instead of buy?
Nothing on the market checks adjective-level framing, editorial voice, or argumentative rigour against a house style. Grammarly checks grammar. Originality.ai detects AI text. Neither audits whether a claim distorts the source. This is bespoke to elite editorial.

Bogdan Vrusias Head AI & Data Eng

Tests: technical depth · architecture · daily working rapport. Pron: BOG-dan VROO-see-ahs.
Why not RAG?
Deliberate choice. Chunked retrieval creates the exact failure mode we're catching — a claim fails grounding because the supporting sentence sat in an unseen chunk. For an audit tool, the LLM needs the full picture. At scale: hybrid — large-context for grounding, retrieval for the voice rules library.
How would this work with Bedrock?
Architecture is model-agnostic. Swap OpenAI for Claude via Bedrock — same JSON schema, same three parallel calls. Bedrock Guardrails handles safety/toxicity, freeing Red Pen judges for editorial-specific checks. I'd test Claude's structured-output compliance before committing.
Why no LangChain?
Don't say "it didn't cooperate." Three parallel SDK calls with strict JSON didn't need an orchestration framework. Pure calls are easier to debug and audit. At scale — caching, routing, cost controls — I'd revisit orchestration.
How do you evaluate the evaluator?
Phase 3: held-out dataset of editor-annotated drafts with known flaws. Run three judges. Track precision and recall per pillar. CI gate — no prompt change ships without passing. Same pattern I used at PageBreak.
What about latency?
15–30s for three parallel calls on a 1500-word article. Acceptable for pre-edit QA. At scale: cache source embeddings, pre-process voice rules, stream so grounding flags appear first.

Josh Muncke VP Gen AI

Tests: strategic fit · group-wide thinking · internal tools vision.
How does this relate to the broader AI platform?
The three-judge pattern generalises. Grounding = checking output against source data. Voice = brand/compliance rules. Rigour = logical consistency. EIU copilot, customer service bots, internal tools — all need a version. The Red Pen is the proof; the platform capability is the eval harness underneath.
We're piloting Claude internally. How do internal tools evolve?
Liz mentioned a decentralised approach — company-wide ChatGPT, Claude pilot starting. Opportunity: shared guardrails for internal use too. If an analyst uses Claude to draft a client brief, same grounding check applies. Platform serves both external products and internal workflows.
What's your view on agentic AI?
Agents multiply the need for guardrails, not reduce it. Every autonomous step is a point where editorial trust can break. The eval harness becomes more critical — you audit agent outputs before they reach a human, not after.

Nelson Nogueira Senior Delivery Lead

Tests: buildability · scope control · sprint hygiene · realistic timelines. Pron: Nelson No-GAIR-ah.
What's your approach to scoping and delivery?
Thin vertical slice. For Red Pen: one judge, one article type, one editor. Ship in 2–3 sprints. Measure. Then widen. I write acceptance criteria before sprint planning — the team shouldn't be guessing what done looks like.
How do you handle scope creep on AI features?
AI features are scope-creep magnets because the model always has an opinion. I scope by output contract, not capability. The judge returns this JSON shape. It flags these categories. Anything outside the schema is out of scope this sprint.
What does your relationship with engineering leads look like?
I own what and why. They own how. We co-own when. At Hilti: weekly 1:1 with the principal engineer, shared roadmap doc, PRDs he shaped before I finalised. Never wrote tickets he hadn't agreed to. When we disagreed, default was prototype and measure.
What's the cost per audit?
Roughly $0.05–0.15 per article depending on source length. Three parallel calls, ~400k context. At 1000 articles/day, $50–150/day. Manageable. At scale: cache source embeddings, reduce context per call.
